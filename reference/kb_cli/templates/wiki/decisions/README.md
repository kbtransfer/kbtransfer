# decisions/

Records of choices made. **Experience — first-class in this KB.**

Every page here captures a single decision with the context that
made it contentious. Future-you or a teammate reading this page
months later should be able to reconstruct not just what was decided
but why the alternatives were rejected.

A good decision page has:

1. **Decision.** One sentence: what was chosen.
2. **Status.** `proposed` | `accepted` | `superseded` | `reversed`.
3. **Context.** The forces at play when the decision was made.
4. **Alternatives considered.** At least two, with reasons rejected.
5. **Consequences.** What this commits you to; what it rules out.
6. **Revisit.** When should this be reviewed again, and under what trigger.

Decision pages distill into the most valuable packs. A reference doc
can be rediscovered from public sources; a decision — why your team
chose X over Y — is private knowledge with genuine market value.
